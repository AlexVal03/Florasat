About Florasat

We are a team passionate about space technology, environmental conservation, and the power of data to create a more sustainable future.

üéØ Our Mission

Florasat was born with the goal of democratizing access to NASA satellite data to monitor global bloom events. We believe that space technology can help farmers, conservationists, researchers, and citizens better understand plant life cycles and their relationship with climate change.

We use satellites like Landsat, MODIS, and VIIRS to detect and visualize blooms around the world, from superblooms in deserts to agricultural crops, providing valuable information for decision-making in agriculture, conservation, and public health.

NASA Space Apps Challenge 2024

Florasat was developed for the NASA Space Apps Challenge 2024 in Valencia, Spain. This project responds to the challenge "BloomWatch: An Earth Observation Application for Global Flowering Phenology", which invited us to create a visual tool to monitor bloom events using NASA Earth observation data.

We use data from missions like Landsat 8/9, MODIS Terra/Aqua, VIIRS Suomi NPP, and resources from the GLOBE Observer program to create an interactive platform that helps understand global bloom patterns and their relationship with climate change.
